# Minimal Home Setup

Bodyweight + resistance bands. No significant equipment.

## Equipment

- Resistance bands (light, medium, heavy)
- Pull-up bar (doorframe)
- Yoga mat
- Optional: Parallettes, ab wheel

## Exercise Library

### Push
- Push-ups (wide, narrow, diamond, decline, archer)
- Pike push-ups
- Handstand push-ups (wall)
- Dips (between chairs)
- Band chest press
- Band shoulder press

### Pull
- Pull-ups / Chin-ups
- Inverted rows (under table)
- Band rows
- Band face pulls
- Band curls

### Legs
- Squats (various stances)
- Pistol squats (assisted → full)
- Lunges (forward, reverse, Bulgarian)
- Nordic curls (assisted)
- Single-leg RDL
- Calf raises (single leg on step)
- Glute bridges

### Core
- Planks (front, side, variations)
- Hollow holds
- L-sits (progressions)
- Ab wheel rollouts
- Mountain climbers
- Leg raises

## Sample Workouts

### Upper Body (35 min)
1. Pull-ups: 4x max
2. Push-ups: 4x max
3. Inverted Rows: 3x12
4. Pike Push-ups: 3x10
5. Band Face Pulls: 3x15
6. Plank: 3x45s

### Lower Body (30 min)
1. Bulgarian Split Squats: 4x8 each
2. Single-leg RDL: 3x10 each
3. Pistol Squat (assisted): 3x5 each
4. Nordic Curl (assisted): 3x6
5. Calf Raises: 3x20

### Full Body (40 min)
1. Pull-ups: 3x max
2. Push-ups: 3x max
3. Bulgarian Split Squats: 3x10 each
4. Inverted Rows: 3x12
5. Pike Push-ups: 3x8
6. Glute Bridges: 3x15
7. Plank: 2x60s

## Progression Methods
- Add reps before harder variation
- Tempo: Slow eccentrics (3-4 sec)
- Paused reps
- 1.5 reps (full + half)
- Band assistance → bodyweight → band resistance

## Notes
- Focus on progression through difficulty
- Perfect for maintenance or skill work
- Can be combined with calisthenics skills
- Great for travel (bands pack easily)
